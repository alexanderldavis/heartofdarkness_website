Kurtz "Possessiveness"

Marlow’s abrupt stop in his story of recalling his journey to the heart of darkness to discuss further details of Kurtz emphasizes the fascination Marlow truly has for Kurtz. Marlow’s interest in Kurtz is sparked by seeing someone so mysterious and so different from the typical man he’s usually surrounded by. The savage lifestyle that Kurtz is living in, opens Marlow’s eyes to the vast unknown on the Congo River and further the darkness found in Kurtz. Kurtz has left civilization and become a possessive man, very greedy with his wealth of ivory in his savage home. The darkness then is not found in just the civilized man, but in human nature itself.
